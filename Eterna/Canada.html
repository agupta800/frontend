Course Fees, Scholarships and Other Expenses

Course Fees

Your expenditure on tuition fees depends on the type of qualification and institution you opt for. To cover this, you will need approximately between CAD 13,000 - CAD 35,000 a year.

Most commonly, courses in humanities, education, and arts are a little cheaper, while subjects such as medicine and engineering are likely to be more expensive. If you wish to study at a postgraduate level, the tuition fee is typically higher, and the costs vary depending on the program. As with most other countries, MBA programs are often the most expensive. The average cost for these programs ranges between CAD 13,000-42,000 a year.

The admission process in Canada for international students is very transparent. The visa application for Canada from Nepal involves payment of a standard fee of C$150 for all the candidates. The admission process varies for every institute. The basics, like the English language proficiency test, remain the same. Tests like IELTS,PTE or TOEFL are the English language proficiency exams that are mandatory for non-native English speakers for admission in Canada.
Most of the colleges and universities along with Canadian immigration require that you must have overall 6.5 but not less than 6.0 in each band score in IELTS and Equivalent in other English proficiency tests. Where ILETS is the most preferred test by Canadian High Commission and educational institutions in Canada, and your academic percentage should be minimum 55%.
A gap shouldn't be a problem so long as you have valid work engagements in your relevant field of study.

Study and Beyond - Life in Canada

Canada offers a vibrant and welcoming international student life, filled with opportunities to engage in sports, the arts, and travel. Canada offers much more than just the hustle and bustle of big-city life. Many universities in Canada allow students to take up part-time jobs, provided they have the necessary work permits, albeit with certain limitations.
Typically, students are allowed to work a maximum of 20 hours per week, which can help manage their expenses during their time as international students in Canada.
When it comes to leisure and entertainment, Canadians indulge in a variety of activities. The vibrant cultural scene includes theatre performances, dining at restaurants, experiencing Omnimax shows, exploring Chinatown, enjoying live gigs, immersing in the atmosphere of the Gay Village, and socialising at pubs and clubs. These experiences contribute to the rich student life in Canada.
Canadian cities are known for their liveliness and progressive mindsets, making them well-suited for student life. The welcoming and inclusive nature of these cities creates an environment where students can thrive and grow while embracing diverse cultures and perspectives.

Total Cost to Study in Canada from Nepal

Expenses in Canada

  1. Tuition Fees
  2. Book Materials
  3. Personal Expenses: 200 - 400 CAD
  4. Accommodations Cost

    There are several housing options available in Canada, including:

    • Student Residences: 250 - 625 CAD per month
    • University-Arranged Home stays: 400 - 800 CAD per month
    • Rent & Share an Apartment: 300 - 700 CAD per month
    • A studio/one-room apartment: CAD 1000 CAD per month

    Housing rents depend on the location and the facilities provided. If you wish to use electricity, water and heating, you will have to pay 150 CAD per month for each of these. If you wish to use the internet, then you will have to pay 50-70 CAD per month for each of these.

  5. Transportations Costs

    Transportation in Canada is inexpensive. A one-way ticket on average costs $3, while a monthly transport pass is equivalent to $90.
    Students can purchase a discounted monthly transport pass, and those between the ages of 18 and 25 receive a 50% discount on a monthly transport pass. A taxi ride in Canada averages about $2 to $ 6 per kilometer.

  6. Health Expenses and Insurance

    Health insurance is mandatory for all international students to study in Canada. The cost is approximately CAD $600 -$800 per year.

  7. Taxes

    International students with a Social Insurance Number (SIN) are allowed to work part-time in Canada. So, if you are working then it is mandatory to pay taxes to the Canadian government.
