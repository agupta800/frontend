USA Admission Process :

Steps to follow for a successful USA Admission Process for International Students:

1. Course Selection:

Choosing the right course is a decisive moment for aspiring students. There are thousands of educational courses to study and it might be a tough job to decide which course to select. For a better understanding of course selection and its scope please get in touch with a career counselor.

2. University Selection:

After you select the course you want to study, search for the universities which offer your selected program. University selection should be done on the basis of various factors such as the resources, amenities, accommodation, fee structure, and employment prospects provided by them. For unbiased information and shortlisting, US universities contact us.

3. Contact Universities Admission Department:

You can contact the US universities for more information regarding their teaching methods, internships, accommodation, fee structure, international students admission information, etc. You can get in contact with them through mail and phone.




4. Check Admission Criteria:

For a hassle-free admission process, check the eligibility criteria, and list of documents required for university admission. The eligibility criteria vary from university to university.

5. Take Admission Tests:

Book an appointment for your tests such as the IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, GRE, GMAT, SAT, OET, etc. 6 months prior to the test date. Learn about the cutoffs and start preparing for the test.

6. Prepare your SOP'S and LOR'S:

while submitting your application form you have to submit an SOP (statement of purpose) and 1-3 LORs (letters of recommendation) to the universities.

  • "The SOP is an essay stating the purpose of your study abroad, your goals and aspirations, and how to intend to contribute to the university and community."
  • "An LOR is a letter which is written by an employer or professor stating the applicants personal personality and professional performance."

7. Fill out the Application Form:

after collecting all the essential documents, SOP and LORs you can now fill in the application forms of all the shortlisted universities. Visit the official websites and pay the application fees online. Additional forms might have to be filled up in case of scholarship applications.

8. Wait for Acceptance Letter:

the status of your application whether you are accepted or rejected will be informed to you on your registered email address.

9. Fee payment:

once you get selected by a university, deposit the admission fees

10. Apply for the US Study Visa:

apply for an F or M visa and a Form I-20.

Types of USA Student Visas

There are three types of Student Visas for you to enter and stay in the US. They are as follows:

F1 Visa

The F1 Visas are issued to students who are willing to pursue an academic program or study the English language at a recognized US college or university. All undergraduate and graduate programs come under the F1 Visa. The F1 status also allows part-time and on-campus employment for students.

J1 Visa-

The J1 Visa is extended to people who are willing to receive practical training and study any vocational courses in the USA. These visas are also for visiting scholars and working professionals who are pursuing an exchange program.

M-1 Visa

The M1 Visas are issued to students who take up a vocational program or non-academic course in the US. Students under these visas are not allowed to work during the course of study.

Documents required to Study in USA

The eligibility criterion for an overseas study aspirant alters with his choice of university and the type of programs. However, there are a few common criteria that apply to every individual applying for a US study program. They are as follows:

  1. Transcripts or records of grades (high school and/or university)
  2. Resume
  3. Two to three years of work (for MBA aspirants)
  4. English language test scores (IELTS, TOEFL, PTE) and US entrance test scores (SAT, GRE, GMAT)
  5. Letter(s) of Recommendation
  6. Statement of Purpose, Personal Statement, Essay(s)
  7. Proof of payment of Application fees
  8. Passport photos

Work Options after your Study in USA :

The USA job market is booming with each passing day. This means that there are huge economic prospects for fresh graduates. Moreover, the American government has framed certain laws which ensure that students can stay back in the USA after graduation for up to 12 months under the Post-Study Work Permit. For STEM graduates, however, it is a 24-month stay-back period. During this period you can search for a job and continue staying in the USA until you get an H1B visa. An H1B visa will ensure that you can work in USA for 6 years. You can eventually apply for a Green Card.

Fact Sheet about the USA :

The USA Culture

The USA is a melting pot of different cultures, races, and ethnicity and the nation takes pride in its diverse culture. The acceptance of people from different backgrounds and cultures creates a productive environment for the students in and out of the campus.

However, there are some Dos and Donts that every international student needs to follow:

Dos in the USA

  • Americans love boasting about their accomplishments. Therefore, talking about your achievements in America is a good way to boost someones confidence.
  • Try to mingle with the local people by engaging yourself in a friendly discussion with them. Americans love small chat and talking about yesterdays football match or the weather.

Donts in the USA

  • Avoid discussing sensitive topics such as civil rights, hardcore politics, religions, etc. These conversations may suddenly lead to a heated discussion thereby putting you in an uncomfortable position.
  • Never try to talk impolitely about the USA. The US people are proud of their country and do not take a word against it.

        FAQ'S :

        Q) What is the academic calendar for universities in the United States?

        A) The academic year for universities in the United States is usually from August to May. Most universities use either the semester system (two terms), the quarter system (students attend three out of four total terms), or the trimester system (three terms).

        Q) What is a GPA?

        A) Grade Point Average (GPA) is a numeric indicator for a student's academic performance which is calculated on a scale of 4.0.

        Q) What is the credit system in U.S. universities?

        A) A credit is a value assigned to each course reflecting the number of hours the class will meet the professor each week.

        Q) What are the popular courses in the USA?

        A) STEM (Sciences, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) subjects are well opted by international students in the USA.

        Q) What is the difference between an F-1 and J-1 visa?

        A) An F-1 visa is a student visa that is needed to study at a college, school, or university in the USA. A J-1 visa is for an exchange student for a temporary stay in the USA.

        Q) Are any scholarships available for international students in the USA?

        A) Yes, there are numerous scholarships available for international students based on merit and proficiency in sports or cultural activities. .

        Q) Can I apply to a university in the United States without an English test such as TOEFL, IELTS, or PTE?

        A) Yes, Most universities will ask you to take one of these English Language tests as part of the application process. Others will provide an opportunity to study English once you are attending a university.

        Q) Are there any age limit to attending USA universities?

        A) Yes, you must be 17 years of age and completed high school to attend US universities.

        Q) How many intakes are there in USA?

        A) There are 3 major intakes in USA. spring, fall and summer.

        Q) What are the stay-back opportunities available?

        A) Indian students can stay back in USA for 1 year after their graduation. STEM graduates can however stay back for upto 3 years.

        Q) Can I work while studying in USA?

        A) Yes, you can work while studying in USA.

        Q) Can I enter USA without IELTS?

        A) Yes you can enter the USA without IELTS. However, there are certain criteria that need to be met.

        Q) Is it possible to crack a visa interview on the first attempt?

        A) Yes, it is possible to crack a visa interview in the first attempt provided you take assistance from a visa counselor.

        Q) Is it a good idea to study in USA?

        A) Yes, study in USA creates a great opportunity and provides all-round development for students.

        Q) What is the difference between undergraduate and graduate courses in the USA?

        A) Undergraduate programs follow high school and lead to an associate (two-year) degree or a bachelor's (four-year) degree. Graduate programs follow a bachelors degree and lead to a masters or doctoral degree.

        Q) What is the difference between state universities and private universities in USA?

        A) US State universities are funded by the state and are larger and less expensive than private universities.

        Q) Which test should I take for study in USA?

        A) You can take any English language test of your choice for a US study.
